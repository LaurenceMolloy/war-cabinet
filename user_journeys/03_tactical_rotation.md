# User Journey: The Great Rotation

**The Question:** *"I've got a big stockpile in the garage, but I'm worried it's all going to expire out there. How do I make sure I'm actually eating the oldest stuff first?"*

A stockpile is only useful if it's fresh. "Tactical Rotation" is the practice of moving older items from your long-term storage (like the Garage Freezer or the Deep Pantry) into your active kitchen cupboards. This guide shows you how the **Quartermaster** automatically tracks these intervals and prompts you when it's time to swap items out.

---

### Step 1: Checking the Rotation Roster
Tap the **Logistics** tab and select the **ROTATION** mode. 
Here, the app identifies batches that have been sitting in a cabinet for longer than their "Rotation Interval" (e.g., 6 months for a freezer).

Items are grouped by cabinet. If a badge says **"OVERDUE"**, that stock has been sitting there too long and needs to be moved to your active kitchen for consumption.

> *[Suggested Image: A screenshot of the Rotation tab, showing a list of items with "DUE" or "OVERDUE" badges.]*

---

### Step 2: Selecting your Targets
1. Browse the list and find the items you're going to move today.
2. **Tap each item** to select it. You'll see a blue highlight appear.
3. For each selected item, the app will suggest a "Target Cabinet" (e.g., "Rotate to: KITCHEN PANTRY"). If you want to move it somewhere else, just tap the destination name to change it.

---

### Step 3: Executing the Move
Once you've actually physically moved the items in your house:
1. Tap the large yellow **MARK ROTATED & RELOCATED** button at the bottom.
2. The app will instantly update the location of all those items in your inventory and reset their "Rotation Clock" to today.

---

### Step 4: Reviewing the Mission (Optional)
If you want to see exactly what you moved today, tap the **SUMMARY** button in the top right. The app maintains a daily log of all tactical movements, so you can always verify where those 10 tins of soup ended up!
